THE FUEL LEDGER
/

A 2 A.M. BUSINESS PLAN — BENGALURU, 2026

A ₹1,335 idea that ends in a courtroom.

Make cheap fuel at home, sell it dear at your own racetrack, keep the gap. It feels like free money at 2 a.m. Walk through it slowly and watch it fall apart.

“What if I make the cheap fuel and sell it through my racetrack? Make at ₹165, sell at ₹1,500, keep ₹1,335 a litre. Synergy. I am a genius.”

THE FOUNDER, LEANING BACK IN HIS CHAIR
01 — THE STARTING POINT

Two half-answers, neither of which works alone

India’s petrol is now E20 — a fifth ethanol — which worries owners of older performance bikes. There are two ways to get cleaner fuel. Each is broken in its own way.

OPTION A

Import race fuel

Reasonably legal to sell for track use. But ocean freight and customs duty roughly quadruple what you pay.

₹700–1,290 / litre — too costly
OPTION B

Brew it at home

Wash the ethanol out of pump petrol and rebuild the octane. Cheap to produce — but you legally cannot sell it.

~₹165 / litre — but unsellable

03 — WHERE IT BREAKS

Seven walls, one after another

i.

There is no bulk discount

No wholesale tap exists for a private buyer. Your input petrol stays at full retail — about ₹111 a litre before you have done anything at all.

ii.

The chemistry works; the price will not move

After solvents, energy and waste, you have spent roughly ₹165 a litre to recreate a fuel that already sits on the pump — only slightly worse.

iii.

It isn’t race fuel

You set out to make competition fuel. What you made is lightly doctored 95-octane pump petrol. That single fact dissolves the whole cover story.

iv.

In law, you are now a refinery

The moment you turn petrol into a blended fuel, the fire, factory, pollution and explosives authorities all classify you as a manufacturer. Your reason for doing it does not matter.

v.

The racetrack covers nothing

The track’s licence was cut for genuine competition fuel. Run your homemade brew through it and all it gives the authorities is a fixed, signposted address to visit.

vi.

The excise wall

Make and sell motor fuel and you owe central excise. Selling off-spec fuel without paying that duty is, plainly, adulteration and tax evasion — squarely criminal.

vii.

The costs stack; they do not share

You have bolted a refinery onto a fuel station onto a racetrack. None of the three subsidises the others. The “synergy” was never there.

06 — THE LIABILITY LEDGER

Every weakness survives, and adds up

01

Fire and explosion

Petrol and toluene vapour, now at commercial volume.

02

Toxic exposure

Aromatic solvents — both chronic and acute harm.

03

Unlicensed manufacture

Explosives, factory, pollution and fire rules all triggered.

04

Off-spec sale

It fails retail specification — that is adulteration.

05

Excise evasion

Selling motor fuel without ever paying the duty.

06

A fixed address

The track makes you easy to find — not safe.

07 — THE WHOLE SAGA

Five escalating schemes, one destination

1

Smuggle fuel across the water by boat

Blocked by customs — and motorcycles do not float.

2

Buy a racetrack just to store it

The venue was never the thing that made the fuel legal.

3

Import the real thing

Reasonably legal, but ₹700–1,290 a litre and a market far too small.

4

De-blend pump petrol yourself

Works chemically, breaks even with XP100 at best, illegal at any scale.

5

Make it and sell it through the track

Combines the costs and the crimes. Nets out to ₹165 a litre and a courtroom.

THE VERDICT

A crore-scale way to recreate the pump price

A criminal manufacturing operation, turning out an inferior fuel you cannot legally sell, from a fixed licensed address, demanding more than fifty crore in capital — all to reach a production cost of ₹165 a litre.

Meanwhile XP100 — a better fuel, needing no plant, no track and no crimes — already sits on the pump at roughly ₹160–165 a litre.

In every version of the plan, the cheapest fuel was the one someone else had already made and already paid the tax on. And the bike was perfectly fine on E20 the entire time.

Satire-flavoured economics — not a build manual, and not legal or financial advice. Making motor fuel means handling volatile, toxic chemicals, and at any real scale it is unlawful for a private person in India. This page exists to show why the idea fails, not how to attempt it.

PART ONE · AN IDEAL-WORLD THOUGHT EXPERIMENT — ONLY THE CHEMISTRY

Can you turn E20 back into ethanol-free petrol? On the chemistry alone — yes.

Set aside cost, scale, hazard and law. Imagine a world where only the molecules matter. The question is simply whether the chemistry cooperates — and it does. Here is exactly what is happening, step by step.

E20 is just petrol with a fifth of it swapped for ethanol. Ethanol loves water and lifts octane. Take both facts seriously, and the path back to ethanol-free petrol is only separation and re-blending.

THE PREMISE, IN ONE BREATH
01 — WHAT E20 ACTUALLY IS

Two liquids sharing one tank

E20 is twenty percent ethanol blended into ordinary petrol. The two components behave very differently — and that difference is the entire reason the blend can be undone.

80%

Gasoline

A mixture of hydrocarbons. Non-polar, so it does not mix with water. It carries most of the fuel’s energy.

NON-POLAR · HYDROPHOBIC
20%

Ethanol

A small polar alcohol, C₂H₅OH. It dissolves freely in water, absorbs moisture from the air, and raises the blend’s octane.

POLAR · WATER-SOLUBLE · HIGH OCTANE

03 — THE PROCESS

Pull the ethanol out, put the octane back

E20→ water wash→ dry→ + octane→ trim volatility→ E0 · 95 RON
i.

Separate the ethanol

Add water. Ethanol is far more soluble in water than in petrol, so it migrates into the water and the two liquids settle into distinct layers. The ethanol leaves with the water.

PRINCIPLE · LIQUID–LIQUID EXTRACTION
ii.

Dry the petrol

The washed petrol now holds a trace of dissolved water. Remove it, and you are back to a clean, water-free hydrocarbon — but one that has lost the octane the ethanol was providing.

RESTORE A WATER-FREE HYDROCARBON
iii.

Rebuild the octane

Ethanol was also doing chemical work: holding up the octane number. With it gone, octane drops. A high-octane aromatic such as toluene restores it — no alcohol required.

PRINCIPLE · OCTANE BLENDING
iv.

Trim the volatility

Ethanol changed how readily the fuel evaporates. Rebalance the lighter and heavier fractions so the vapour pressure lands back in the ordinary range for petrol.

PRINCIPLE · VAPOUR PRESSURE (RVP)
04 — THE OCTANE QUESTION

05 — THE CONCEPTS

The handful of ideas doing the work

01

Octane (RON)

A fuel’s resistance to knock. Higher numbers tolerate higher compression without igniting early.

02

Knock

Uncontrolled, premature combustion that stresses an engine. Octane is what holds it off.

03

Hygroscopic

Ethanol pulls water out of the air. It is why ethanol blends and moisture do not get along.

04

Liquid–liquid extraction

Using one liquid (water) to pull a compound (ethanol) out of another liquid (petrol).

05

Reid vapour pressure

How readily fuel evaporates. Too high and it vapour-locks; too low and it will not cold-start.

06

Aromatics

Ring-shaped hydrocarbons such as toluene, naturally high in octane.

THE IDEAL CASE

On the chemistry alone, the loop closes

Strip out a solvent that happens to dissolve in water. Replace the octane it was carrying with an aromatic that is not an alcohol. Rebalance how the mixture evaporates. Every one of these is a standard, well-understood operation.

Take away every question of cost, scale, hazard and law — leave only the molecules on the bench — and it resolves cleanly: E20 goes in, ethanol-free petrol near 95 octane comes out.

The science was never the obstacle. Remove ethanol’s water-loving trick and the octane it carried, and what remains is ordinary petrol — plus the octane you blend back in.

PART TWO · FROM THE BENCH TO A BUSINESS

The chemistry is only half the question. Could you actually sell it?

Forget brewing it. Suppose you simply import genuine competition fuel and sell it, legally, through a racetrack you already own. Here is whether that holds up as a business — demand, rules, costs and all.

Sell unleaded competition fuel for track use — the same lane existing race-fuel vendors live in. What a customer does after the gate is their call, not the track’s.

THE MODEL, STATED HONESTLY
B1 — THE DEMAND

A real pain, looking for a fix

From April 2026 all Indian petrol is E20 at 95 RON minimum — nationwide, no owner opt-out, and the Supreme Court declined a plea to guarantee ethanol-free supply. Trouble is, plenty of pre-2023 performance bikes were only ever rated for E10.

01

Kawasaki Z900 / Z900 RS

Rated E10 max, unleaded only — never leaded.

02

Kawasaki Ninja 1000 SX (pre-2023)

E10 max — the very owner who first raised the alarm.

03

KTM supermoto / SMC class

Rated E10 from model year 2000 onward.

04

Japanese inline-fours, 2007–2022

Typical of the segment — all E10-rated.

The risk isn’t a sudden breakdown — it’s long-term wear to injectors, pumps and seals, a 3–7% mileage drop, and moisture pickup in storage.

B2 — THE MODEL

The clean core, and the line you don’t cross

The legal core

A PESO-licensed installation stocks unleaded, low/zero-ethanol competition fuel and sells it for track and off-road use. Anchor it with track days and sanctioned events.

LAWFUL · ESTABLISHED LANE

The grey zone

A rider who fills up “for the track” and then heads onto the road is making a personal choice. Keep the fuel unleaded, and never market it as a road-fuel workaround.

CUSTOMER’S CALL · NEVER ADVERTISE
B3 — THE RULE-BOOKS

Five regimes you have to satisfy

01

PESO storage & dispensing

Past ~30 litres you need a licence, approved tanks, flameproof electricals and fire safety.

02

Import permission & customs

Imported race fuel needs PESO import permission plus customs clearance — and heavy duty.

03

BIS retail spec

Race fuel isn’t road-legal spec, so it may only be sold for competition / off-road use.

04

Leaded-petrol road ban

Stay unleaded and the customer never trips the one clear personal offence.

05

Fuel excise

India taxes fuel at the gate by product, not by use — so there’s no red-diesel-style trap for petrol.

B6 — THE RISKS

Where it can go wrong

01

Marketing drift

Imply road use and the whole legal core collapses.

02

Leaded fuel

Stocking it pushes a road offence onto your customers — stay unleaded.

03

Customs end-use

Sporting-use import concessions can bite if fuel is diverted; price at full duty.

04

Safety & liability

Class-A petroleum storage is high-hazard, with serious fire and insurance exposure.

05

Policy drift

E22–E30 and flex-fuel are coming; the anxiety window may narrow over time.

B7 — THE BIGGER PLAY

The cleaner business hiding inside

Target the anxiety, not the molecule, and the market gets larger while the licensing burden disappears.

01

Ethanol-mitigation products

Corrosion inhibitors, water-dispersants, ethanol-resistant lines and seals, tank treatments. No PESO, no BIS conflict.

02

Premium vehicle care & storage

Climate-controlled storage and fuel management for collectible bikes — the owners with both the worry and the budget.

03

Licensed track-day supply

Partner with circuits and FMSCI events so genuine on-track demand carries the fuel line.

THE BOTTOM LINE

Viable — but only bolted onto a track you already own

As a small, disciplined add-on, an unleaded competition-fuel line is plausible and legal. As a reason to build a circuit or chase road-fuel demand, it fails: the market is tiny, the safety and compliance load is heavy, and the one big demand pool — road users — is exactly the one you cannot advertise to.

India taxes fuel at the gate, has no red-diesel trap, and doesn’t test fuel roadside — so unleaded competition fuel, your own track, and a customer who simply leaves the gate is about as clean as Indian fuel law allows.

The catch was never legal. It’s commercial: the market you can lawfully serve is a sliver of the market that actually wants it.
